Academic Field Research 1.5 — Specialism

Posted on 2017-04-29 |

THIS RESEARCH IS BASED ON F.R. 1.0, FOR MORE INFORMATION PLEASE VISIT Field Research 1.0 — with ACE steps.

In this research, I’d love to put all my attention on the most important area, which was brought up during last Weekly Team Meeting and I poorly answered to.

Because only Alliance Manchester Business School offers a clear course structure which states there are 2 routes for prospective students to take, I decide to focus mainly on its specialism routes.

Semester 1

It’s a semester composed with four mandatory taught course units. Critical Marketing, Marketing Implementation, Marketing and Communications Professional Analysis, Strategic Marketing and New Product Development are all about fundamental theories and principles of marketing.

Semester 2

In Semester 2, we’ve got two routes to choose from. Route 1 is the general route and Route 2 is the specialism route. The major difference between these two is if there is a core module which makes an elective course compulsory.

There are five specialisms in total, which are B2B, Consumer Behavior, International, Retailing and Services.

After reading into the detailed course description for every single course unit, I think general is better for me.

In a general route, I think I will take Consumer Behavior, e-Business and Digital Technologies for Marketing. These three units can really support my career goal.

Summer Research and Dissertation

I believe I will still want to work for a high-tech company like Apple or Microsoft, especially after taking two semesters’ courses. So the summer research and dissertation must be related to how “e-everything” has changed our life dramatically.

Next…

Blythe, J. (2014), Consumer Behaviour, 2nd Edition, Sage.

Chaffey, Dave, Digital Business and E-Commerce Management, Pearson Education Ltd, 6th edn, 2014.

These can enhance my academic background in a way, and help me get a better vision about the program I am going to take.
