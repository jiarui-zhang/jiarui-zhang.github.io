ACE — Chap.I Alumni-One

Posted on 2017-04-27 |

Hi, there! It’s been a few days since my last post. Let’s dig a little deeper for the mostly-positive rated ACE method. Here we’ll start with Alumni.

It may take around 6 minutes for you to read the following great ideas. If you don’t have that much time, I’m glad to introduce the RSS subscribe, which will be ready when I officially upload this article.

Session One — Look Around You

Have you really considered yourself as the alumni of your dream schools after reading my Introduction To ACE? You may have a bunch of interesting people in your mind, dreaming of becoming one of them. But after a super wonderful workshop meeting, new ideas just flushed in. And this seems to be a better beginning for the whole ACE process — Just take a break and look around you.

Do you know them? I bet you know some. Pick up one randomly and think one critical question: What is his/her occupation? A professor? A manager? A housekeeper? A waiter? A policewoman…? This works best if you are doing your internship.

  1. Take your courage, step forward, show your smile and ask him/her “where did you graduate from?” After knowing his/her legendary life course, you may find the major he/she took doesn’t fit with what he/she is doing. You may find dozens of turning points stats with “I could have, you know”, remember them.
  2. Repeat the last process again and again with 10 different people you are familiar with, make sure they will share the true life tracks with you.
  3. Review the precious information you’ve collected. Find those who have the least turning points, talk to them again. Ask them one last question: Are you happy with your job?

You must have noticed that I didn’t mention about specific professions no more. The reason is obvious, you are only starting to recognize some alumni. I don’t know what conclusion you may get, but I’m sure you must have got the primary impression of the invisible link between major and work.

During this session, two advice should be taken into consideration.

(1) Do not argue because the 10 people you find is of great limitation, they are who you are.

(2) Do not find too many of them with the same occupation and same level of studying, regardless your potential profession and level degree. Just remember, this is for general.

What’s your great findings? Share with me now! Do you still believe the alumni really matters? Are those with a higher education more stable when choosing their jobs? Are they deeply affected by their schools? Who do you want to be alumnus with? Do you still have faith in your dream school or preferred major?
